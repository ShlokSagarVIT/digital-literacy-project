CYBERCRIME CASE STUDY: UPI / ONLINE PAYMENT FRAUD

UPI fraud is a common cybercrime in India where attackers trick users
into sending money or sharing sensitive details. A typical scenario
involves a victim receiving a phone call or message claiming to be from
a bank, delivery service, or customer support. The attacker asks the
victim to click a link or scan a QR code, claiming it is required to
receive money or resolve an issue. In reality, scanning the QR code or
approving a collect request results in money being debited from the
victim’s account.

In many cases, attackers also create fake payment pages or apps that
look identical to legitimate ones. Victims are often targeted through
social media, SMS, or phone calls, especially students and individuals
unfamiliar with digital payment security practices.

The consequences include financial loss, unauthorized transactions, and
exposure of personal and banking information. Recovery can be difficult
if the fraud is not reported immediately. This highlights the importance
of awareness and cautious online behavior while using digital payment
systems.
